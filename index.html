Top Dinos

T-Rex

Tyrannosaurus is a genus of large theropod dinosaur.
The type species Tyrannosaurus rex is one of the best represented theropods.
It lived throughout what is now western North America, on what was then an island continent known as Laramidia.
Tyrannosaurus measures up to 13 m in length, and 8.8 tonnes in mass.

Other Dinos

Triceratops

Triceratops is a genus of chasmosaurine ceratopsian dinosaur.
Name Triceratops, which means 'three-horned face', is derived from the Greek langage.
Triceratops is one of the most recognizable of all dinosaurs and the best-known ceratopsian.
It was also one of the largest, up to 8–9 metres long and 5–9 metric tons in body mass.

More Popular Dinos

Stegosaurus

Stegosaurus is a genus of herbivorous, four-legged, armored dinosaur from the Late Jurassic, characterized by the distinctive kite-shaped upright plates along their backs and spikes on their tails.

Diplodocus

Diplodocus was a genus of diplodocid sauropod dinosaurs.Among the best-known sauropods, Diplodocus were very large, long-necked, quadrupedal animals, with long, whip-like tails.

Ankylosaurus

Ankylosaurus is a genus of armored dinosaur.Ankylosaurus is estimated to have been between 6 and 8 meters long and to have weighed between 4.8 and 8 metric tons.
